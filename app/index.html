Hi,

I’m Sara. I’m a Fashion Designer with a focus on Bag design. I have a passion for innovative pattercutting, functional design and creativity.

I completed my Bachelor Degree in Fashion Design at IUAV, University of Venice in 2016. During my studies I covered both Menswear and Womenswear and developed a strong skill for both design and pattern cutting .I explored various textile, knitt and print techniques. I have also attended an internship at Alessio Zinato ,as a Product Assistant and Pattern Maker, for the brand’s meanswear collection. For my latest academic projects, I focused especially on leather products developing eight accessories collections which were showcased and exhibited during Fashion at IUAV.

In March 2017 I was one of twelve designers worldwide, selected to attend a 3 month bag design and accessories development course ,promoted by IUAV University of Venice in collaboration with Bottega Veneta. During this time I had the opportunity to develop a capsule collection under the mentorship of Bottega Veneta's technicians . This unreal experience was aimed at the development of fully contemporary professionals who are able to create connections between the technical know-how and invention process of the craft, pattern making and the creative vision of the designer.

During these experiences I learned the value of team-playing, strict timing and short schedules. In these fast paced environments I learnt to be a quick learner and sometimes to find unexpected solutions.

Firefighters is a project born during the Master of Bag Design and Accessory Development promoted by IUAV in collaboration with Bottega Veneta.

The search for an aesthetic reference led me to study the American firefighters. I analyzed the uniforms and the various elements that compose them, developing concepts like added elements, hooks, belts and carabiners. These elements are placed in the bags like hanging elements of the small pockets.

I worked on the three-dimensional part of the helmet that crosses the structure like a rib. It becomes the framework element of both bags, accompanying the movements and keeping the curvature.

Versatility and usefulness were a priority. The backpack should be carried with the handle on the shoulder, like a pack or a shoulder strap. The messenger becomes furnished of different type of pockets, with large spaces directly accessible from the outside.

NEXT PROJECT

Proud To Be Black

The Black Panther Party was the main source of inspiration for the birth and development of this project.

Following a historical and visual study of the theme, the main concepts became clear. The goal was to communicate the fundamental themes: strength, fight, political message. I reworked political posters,related to crucial events of that period, printing them on different textures to obtain a new reading key.

To strengthen the accessories, I used military volumes, like backpacks, shoulder bags and holdall. The jackets look like armour due to the reinforced padding and raw finishing. Macro prints met materials such as leather, jacron and jeans.

The work ended with the presentation of eight accessories that walk the catwalk at Fashion at IUAV 2016.

Uppercut

Uppercut purpose was to create accessories inspired by the idea of movement.

I decided to face this theme reading the movement as a political and moral. So I started an historical research on the subject. I choose the one it felt the closest to me: the suffragette. However, during this study I realized that I needed to strengthen this concept by bringing back a series of physical elements that could help me develop volumes.

I opted for the idea of hybridization thanks to a parallel search whithin the world of boxing women of the ‘30s. This allowed me to bring the idea of movement from the abstract to the concrete. I could then decide materials, shapes, volumes, and colors.

I focused on finishes and details. During this phase I had the chance to experience weaves and finishes. I wanted to hybridize the brandy leather with more technical fabrics. I then created a new material, made by a layer of technical microbase, one of jute and one of synthetic fabric. In some seams I inserted a filetto worked with a waxed lacing piping. This has become the theme of design. For this reason also the puller was replaced with a handmade one.

The care of every detail was an indispensable principle that followed every stage of design.

NEXT PROJECT

Infected Roots

Infected Roots was developed from a feeling that, from inside, becomes external through the creation of an object.

I started from a deep analysis of the unconscious. Once I identified shapes and volumes, I looked for a fabric that could have the technical features that perfectly suited the idea of the project.

After several researches and samples, I decided to use a melange wool that was worked with large knitting needles for the construction of the braid structure.

The braid was joined by thinner woolen cord crotchetted , arrange to create a grid that trapped the body. The body is locked in the gown, impending the fluid movements.

NEXT PROJECT

(s)perduta

(S)perduta is a photographic project developed in team to be a storytelling without words.

We were required to work in groups and focus especially on the styling operation. I decided to face this new experience not from a concept, but by doing a research for clothes who themselves told a story. By visiting different thrift shop, we found pieces that have given us a feeling that could be communicated in the way we were thinking.

She wanders for this abandoned house.
Her face is never seen, as a specter without identity, a figure without memory
trying to find herself wearing the clothes she finds in that home, living in the environments and
trying to fit them but without succeeding.

This feeling of non-belonging remains. We made the idea of a ghost through blurry photos, motionless and plastic poses.
